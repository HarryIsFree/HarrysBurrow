The Burrow

The burrow is named after Weasleys' Burrow, ugly house of the impoverished Weasley family.


The Weasley family in Egypt
Adopted from the film:
Harry Poter and the Prisoner of Azkaban

As you can see in the photo above, Mr&Mrs Weasley obviously fail on their birth control plan. They have 7 children, which is one of the reasons accounting for their poverty. The whole family used to live in a house called the Burrow. It is named this way because the house is ugly and twisted in its outlook. The Weasleys couldn't afford to buy a new house or decorate it a lit bit. They end up calling it The Burrow which not only vividly describes the house but also shows an positive attitude toward poor financial status of the family.Well, now let's have a look at the actual burrow.


The Burrow
Adopted from fanpop.com

This is the place Harry hides from his uncle and aunt's abuse. This is the place where friendship turns into a life time support. This is the place where a clock on the wall points not to time but to the safety of each family member. The family is large and poor, but they never hesitate to hug and help whoever is in need. The house is ugly and small, but love makes it expand to a scale beyond capability of language. Now, the Burrow will be my home on the Internet, it won't be as ugly(^^), but is sure to be lovable.
